The Trump Administration's Impact on Immigration

The Trump Administration's first term was wrought with controversial immigration policies. His hallmark campaign promise of
a border wall was meant to secure the country from illegal immigration from Mexico. This was coupled with a harsh deportation
enforcement that resulted in separating families, housing them in facilities Former First Lady Laura Bush compared to the
internment camps used for Japanese-Americans during WW2. The administration attempted to enact a ban on immigration from
13 Muslim-majority countries.

Within the first year of his second term, Trump has resumed his aggressive tactics with regards to immigration. ICE has begun
an aggressive deportation campaign, and the "Big Beautiful Bill" expands funding for ICE and border security to $170 billion.
The Trump administration's tariff policy has also impacted tourism, seeing a 22%, 17%, and 24% reduction in Q1 of 2025 from
Canada, Mexico, and Germany respectively.

So the question is:
What impact has the second Trump Administration had on immigration to the U.S.?
